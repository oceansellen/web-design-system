Typography

Typography is more than just choosing pretty fonts; it's a crucial element that shapes how users interact with content. Typography also influences perceptions and determines the overall effectiveness of a digital product.

Over time, users will begin to associate a site's typeface with its brand identity. Therefore, it's important to keep typography consistent and well planned. Some other aspects of typography to consider include:

  • Readability of typefaces and sizes
  • Visual hierarchy
  • Consistency across all pages
  • Accessibility for all users

User Experience (UX) Design

User experience (UX) design is a collection of techniques that can be used to ensure the most optimal interaction between human and computer.

Simple and clear is the name of the game.

As an example, buttons communicate calls to action to the user and allow users to interact with web pages in a variety of ways. Here, see three different types of buttons: a flat button that's not interactive, a slightly more realistic button with a shadow, and an even more realistic button that hovers and looks 3D.

There are many other principles to good UX design. Figma has some helpful tips and tricks for solid UX/UI design principles.
